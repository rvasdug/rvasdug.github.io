### Description
Creating a highly available and scalable user interface is now easier than ever thanks to client side JavaScript libraries such as React, Angular and Vue that request their data from a microservice. This session will present Ballyhoo!, a Jeopardy-like question and answer multiplayer game; playable in the browser and modeled after real applications found in the wild. We will explore possible user interface pitfalls and craft our own exploit as a browser extension. Whether you are interested in creating a user interface with microservices, provide additional functionality as a browser extension, or enjoy poking around the Internet, Ballyhoo! is guaranteed to be fun and informational.

### Speaker: Robbie Biesser
Robbie Biesser is a recent graduate of ECPI University in Richmond, now holding a Bachelor's Degree in Software Development, but has gained his knowledge as an independent researcher and software developer for over ten years. He is currently looking to join a motivated team in the Richmond area where he can share his passion for software development.
